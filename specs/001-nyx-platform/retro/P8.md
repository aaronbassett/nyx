### Fixed (2 HIGH money-critical + 3 top-up UX)
- **H1 (Opus) — amount column width < contract mint cap.** Postgres `bigint` (2^63−1) is NARROWER than the contract's `Uint<64>` mint cap (2^64−1). A deposit in the gap mints on-chain but `creditDeposit` throws `22003` → ref never flipped → **funds stranded**; and `SUM(amount)::bigint` overflows once cumulative credits pass 2^63−1 → account frozen. **Fix:** migration 0002 widens `ledger_entries.amount`/`deposit_refs.expected_amount`/`orphan_deposits.amount` to `numeric(40,0)` (full Uint128); fold `::bigint`→`::text`; `preregister` rejects amount > 2^64−1. (reconcile_runs.drift/burn_amount stay bigint — Story 10 should widen too.)
- **H2 (both) — deposit-credit exactly-once had NO DB constraint** (only account-row FOR UPDATE + NOT EXISTS; safe under READ COMMITTED, DOUBLE-CREDITS under REPEATABLE READ/SERIALIZABLE). **Fix:** migration 0002 adds `CREATE UNIQUE INDEX ... ON ledger_entries(ref) WHERE kind='deposit_credit'`; `creditDeposit` treats `23505` as already-credited → exactly-once is now structural under ANY isolation. + a real Promise.all concurrency pg test.
- **M1/M2/L3 (top-up):** poll-credit relabelled "Requested" (not "Deposited") + a late `ledger:update` upgrades to server balances (was showing the client-typed amount as server truth under EC-28); expiry-after-tx-submit no longer offers "start a new top-up" (double-deposit footgun); synchronous in-flight latch on `submit` (render-timing-independent).
